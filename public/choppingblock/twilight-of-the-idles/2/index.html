There's no place like home
( Page 2 of 3 ) : Twilight of the Idles, by Rory Tolan

post a comment.

Outraged? Horrified?

Tell us Why.

Every neurosis has a diagnosis, and the pedophile in her reading chair is no real riddle on the psychoanalyst’s couch. For the predators of Edward Cullen, the adult life starts with a shove on the shoulders and a creaking in the cot and a dream of always waking to roses. But then the belly swells and the bosom sags, its eyes too dilated, cracked, and purple to mesmerize; and a crook in the cradle makes a stink in the night for years until his swaddle surrounds still another. The husband sleeps with his work, life begins to ladle its yawns, and what lingers of love is littered among the rapist rompings of Lifetime. It is laid down among those seedy submissions as well as the pubescent pulsations of Twilight, whose fictions the desperate housewife fondles while her husband makes love to his labor.

On the website known as cafemom.com, a popular community among such has-beens and never-wills, a coven called “twilight obsessed moms!” shows just how many homemakers wish a boy named Edward Cullen could deflower them once more. Here, the obsolescent stalks the adolescent with a communal encouragement: steadily growing, its membership now comprises 3,775 mothers, most of whom are middle-aged and handfuls of whom already walk in the middle of their cleavage.

Some of the members despair of their pedophilia. “I don’t think our husbands will ever understand,” one writes, expressing a common secret.

Others embrace their nostalgic desire to copulate with children. “Nothing to be ashamed of,” says another mother (Her and others’ grammar, except in capitalization, has been preserved). “We all need [a] thing to make our lives a little better, no matter what it is.” (Notice, here, the hint of a sociopath’s snobbery.) “So what if it is a 17 yr old virgin vampire or a 15 yr old werewolf that gets our blood boiling[?] At least it lets us know that we are alive!!! Besides its not like ur the only one whose obsessed[:] there’s a whole bunch of us.”

There are, for example, the merchandise-minded mommies who hope to paste onto the bedside wall a six-foot sticker of Edward’s silhouette, available for purchase on the online stores of opportunists everywhere. “Ohhh…. I want one,” says a mother of two from Oklahoma, who is obese and “Christian.” (To insinuate her cruel intentions, she ends her confession with a winking emoticon.)

“Sexy,” responds a sister in vice, here a Texan mother of one, whose profile reveals that she stays home all day, is “stressed,” and has developed an interest in “dining out.” She asks whether it would be appropriate to buy a second sticker, of Jacob Black, the fifteen-year-old werewolf, so that she may juggle her partners as it pleases her. “I just turn over! Woooo love it!” (Leaving less to the imagination than her Oklahoman accomplice, this straight shooter’s endgame is an emoticon unfurling its tongue for a money shot.)

Because the onanist adolescent is not abundant of sexual stamina, the conversation regularly runs to the bookend of sex: the bathroom. “I know I’m discusting,” says an uneducated mother of one, but “do you girlies ever wonder … [whether] vampires also go to the restroom … since they dont eat they just drink blood? I wontder if they really do go [to the] restroom [or] if it’s just a bunch of blood.”

“In Breaking Dawn,” replies a more trained reader, “Bella makes a reference to that very notion.  She said after she was turned [to a vampire] and Edward and her spent [their] first night in their cottage … that they wouldn’t even have to stop making love long enough to use the bathroom cuz they didn’t need to.”
