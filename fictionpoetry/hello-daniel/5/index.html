There's no place like home
( Page 5 of 5 ) : Hello, Daniel, by Paula Carter

We hear something then. A siren. We both listen to it pass, mixing with the wind. She bites at her fingers. I figure she is supposed to be in school. We watch the high-rise together for a while. Someone opens a curtain high on the top floor, but we can’t see anything but the change from white to black.

“Why are you waiting for your father?” she asks.

“Because he wants me to,” I say.

“Hum,” she says.

My father is a small man and has a thick wrinkle on his forehead between his eyes. He will walk here from the subway, each footstep discreet, a kind of clomping, and he will not turn his head to look at the houses but rather keep straight on until he arrives. He will leave his office and get on the subway at 6:27 and arrive here at 6:57. My father will say, “Hello, Daniel”; and the way he will say it will make it sound as if he does not know who I am.

“His name is Z,” she says.

“What?”

“My bird. Short for Zorro,” she says. “Would you like to come in and see him?” she asks.

This does not feel like the right thing to do, with such a young girl, so I say no. But I would like to.

After a while I say, “Maybe we should go in.”

“Maybe,” she says; but neither of us moves. We both just sit there looking at the hundreds of windows behind which there are hundreds of sick people. Without looking at me, she touches my arm, and we stay like that.

Later, I say that she really should go in. It is no longer afternoon.

“When I see you now will you say hello to me?” she asks.

“Yes, I will.”

“I will say hello to you, too,” she says. She nods, smiling and satisfied.

I recross the line of boxelder bugs and turn on the lights in the kitchen because it is starting to get dark. The egg has split open. The bright yellow inside is like a bull’s eye, a target, and I put my finger in and touch it.

My father will be here soon. He will stand in the entryway and wait for me to get my coat. He will put his hands in his pockets. His voice will boom through the house and I will bow a little. If the bird beeps, my father will pretend he does not hear it. But I will hear it and I will know.
